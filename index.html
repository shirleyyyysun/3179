SARS (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome)

"Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus, referred to as SARS coronavirus or SARS virus, is a subtype of the severe acute respiratory syndrome-associated coronavirus species of the genus B coronavirus in the family Coronaviridae, which can cause severe acute respiratory syndrome. on April 16, 2003, the World Health Organization issued a press release confirming that SARS coronavirus is the causative agent of SARS."

SARS Cases Global Distribution by Map

The viral disease first appeared in Southern China in November, 2002 and spread to more than 24 countries in Asia, Europe, North America, and South America. China is the top for number of reported cases. As you can see from the map, Asia is the main area of SARS spread.

SARS Cases by Gender

Overall, there are more confirmed cases in men than in women.

Thus, among SARS patients, males may be more severely affected by the disease than females are.

This finding could be related to a nonuniform case definition of SARS disease, a different treatment regimen, a past smoking history, work-environment factors, or gender-specific immune-defense factors, for instance.

SARS Cases Trending

SARS was first reported in Asia in February 2003.

The SARS pandemic was eventually brought under control in July 2003

Since 2004, there have not been any known cases of SARS reported anywhere in the world.

SARS Recovered Cases Trending

The first recovered case was found in 10th April 2003.

The trend was similar to the confirmed diagnosis, both becoming stable and leveling off from mid-July.

SARS Cases & Death

The number of confirmed cases and deaths was significantly higher in Asia and North America than in other continent. In contrast, Hong Kong has a larger proportion of population to reported cases.
